Our Research

Introduction

Serious games have emerged as a significant development in the realms of military, finance, medical and education, utilising digital technology to create interactive environments for teaching, training, and informing. These games combine instructional material with game mechanics, allowing users to exercise real-world skills, make decisions, and experience the consequences of those decisions in a controlled environment.

How are these games implemented?

Serious games provide a platform for personnel to practise and refine their skills in a risk-free environment. By recreating combat scenarios, crisis management situations, or intelligence tasks, serious games allow trainees to make decisions and experience the consequences without real-world repercussions. This enables them to learn from their mistakes and develop effective strategies in a controlled setting.
Serious games also offer cost-efficiency benefits. They reduce the need for physical resources such as fuel or equipment, minimising wear and tear on expensive equipment. Furthermore, serious games provide a flexible training environment where scenarios can be easily changed and adapted to meet specific training objectives. This allows for a broad range of training opportunities and the ability to address different skill levels and learning needs.
