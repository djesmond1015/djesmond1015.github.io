Our Research

Introduction

Serious games have emerged as a significant development in the realms of military, finance, medical and education, utilising digital technology to create interactive environments for teaching, training, and informing. These games combine instructional material with game mechanics, allowing users to exercise real-world skills, make decisions, and experience the consequences of those decisions in a controlled environment.

How are these games implemented?

Serious games provide a platform for personnel to practise and refine their skills in a risk-free environment. By recreating combat scenarios, crisis management situations, or intelligence tasks, serious games allow trainees to make decisions and experience the consequences without real-world repercussions. This enables them to learn from their mistakes and develop effective strategies in a controlled setting.
Serious games also offer cost-efficiency benefits. They reduce the need for physical resources such as fuel or equipment, minimising wear and tear on expensive equipment. Furthermore, serious games provide a flexible training environment where scenarios can be easily changed and adapted to meet specific training objectives. This allows for a broad range of training opportunities and the ability to address different skill levels and learning needs.
Serious games are implemented in the business and finance sector in various way. Serious games are widely used for corporate training programs. They offer employees an interactive and risk-free environment to learn and practice skills related to finance, management, leadership, and decision-making.
Financial institutions and businesses utilise serious games to enhance employees' financial literacy, investment analysis, and risk management skills. Some companies employ serious games as marketing tools to engage customers and promote financial products or services. Gamification elements can make financial tasks more appealing and accessible.
Serious games are increasingly being employed in the medical domain to enhance both medical education and patient care. Firstly, these games offer a dynamic and engaging platform for medical students and professionals to practice complex surgical procedures and diagnostic skills in a risk-free virtual environment. Such simulations provide hands-on experience and help improve decision-making abilities.
Secondly, serious games are utilized for patient rehabilitation and therapy, particularly in cases of physical and cognitive impairments. These games can motivate patients to adhere to their treatment plans and aid in the recovery process by offering enjoyable and interactive exercises that promote physical activity, cognitive stimulation, and emotional well-being.
Serious games are incorporated into education by aligning them with curriculum goals and creating educational content within the games. Curriculum integration involves mapping learning objectives, fitting the game into lesson plans, and using it for assessment.
Content creation entails designing pedagogically sound materials, using engaging narratives and scenarios, ensuring real-world relevance, and incorporating multimedia elements. This content should progressively challenge students and be accessible to all. Continuous feedback and updates are essential for maintaining the games' educational effectiveness.

What are the challenges?

Implementing serious games for all purposes faces several challenges.

One challenge is the limited availability of serious games that specifically focus on medication safety and education. Many existing serious games in the healthcare domain primarily address medication adherence rather than medication safety. This gap in the literature indicates the need for more serious games that incorporate medication safety components.

Another challenge is the need for more research on the efficacy and usability of medication-focused serious games. Existing studies often have small sample sizes, limiting the generalizability of the findings. Additionally, only some serious games describe the extent to which theoretical frameworks are incorporated into game design, suggesting an area for improvement in the literature.

The assessment and evaluation of serious games also pose challenges. Clinicians and educators may lack the necessary knowledge and tools to assess the safety and effectiveness of serious games. Information on individual games can be challenging to find, and studies on serious games' validity and effectiveness remain scarce.

Furthermore, there may be resistance or scepticism towards using serious games in specific contexts, including the military and healthcare. Concerns about data safety and privacy can also hinder the practical application of serious games in these domains.

How will they develop in the future?

In the future, serious games are poised to undergo a remarkable evolution, driven by advancements in technology and a deeper understanding of human learning and engagement.

Virtual Reality (VR) and Augmented Reality (AR) will play pivotal roles, revolutionizing the immersive potential of serious games. VR will enable users to step into realistic, simulated environments for experiential learning, while AR will blend digital information with the real world, creating new possibilities for contextual education and training.

Personalization will become increasingly sophisticated, leveraging AI and data analytics to tailor serious games to individual learner profiles, ensuring that content adapts to their unique needs and preferences. Moreover, the integration of gamification elements, such as leaderboards and rewards, will motivate continuous engagement and skill development.

As technology continues to advance, the future of serious games holds the promise of highly effective, personalized, and immersive learning experiences that empower individuals to acquire knowledge and skills in unprecedented ways.
